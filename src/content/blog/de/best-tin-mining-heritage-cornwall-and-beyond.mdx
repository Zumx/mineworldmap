Tin was mined in Cornwall for four thousand years and shipped to the Mediterranean by phoenicians. The Cornish tradition spread worldwide via emigrant 'Cousin Jacks'.

## Geevor Tin Mine, Cornwall (UNESCO)

Closed in 1990, now a full visitor mine with engine house, mill and unter Tage tour.

## Levant Mine and Beam Engine, Cornwall (UNESCO)

An 1840 beam engine still running on steam, used to wind men and ore.

## Botallack Mine, Cornwall

Spectacular cliff-edge engine houses on the Atlantic; the diagonal Crowns shaft ran out under the sea.

## Wheal Martyn, Cornwall

China-clay heritage — a related Cornish extractive industry.

## Real del Monte, Mexico

Hidalgo tin-and-silver district founded by Cornish miners; pasties still on every café menu.

## Mount Bischoff, Tasmania

Once the world's largest tin mine; small museum at Waratah.

## Phuket Mining Museum, Thailand

Once the world's tin centre; museum in a former mining manor.

## Bangka, Indonesia

Centuries of tin extraction with Chinese-Hakka community heritage.

## Yunnan Gejiu Tin Museum, China

Yunnanese tin heritage in a regional museum.

## Erzgebirge Tin Workings, Saxony (UNESCO)

Trans-boundary mining landscape inscribed with Czech sites in 2019.

## Tourplanung

Cornwall is the spiritual home — combine Geevor, Levant and Botallack into a single coastal day. Die meisten Stätten verfügen über Besucherzentren open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
