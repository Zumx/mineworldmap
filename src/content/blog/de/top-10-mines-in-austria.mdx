Das alpine Salz- und Silbererbe Österreichs and silver heritage dates back to the Iron Age Hallstatt culture — the world's first major industrial mining tradition — and continues today as living tourism.

## Salzwelten Hallstatt

The world's oldest salt mine, in continuous operation for over 7000 years; the prehistoric tour includes a 64-metre miners' slide.

## Salzwelten Salzburg (Dürrnberg)

A Celtic and medieval salt mine where visitors cross an unter Tage brine lake by boat.

## Schwaz Silver Mine, Tyrol

Once the largest silver mine in 16th-century Europe; train descent into 800 Metern of horizontal gallery.

## Schaubergwerk Mauterndorf

A small Salzburg silver mine with a preserved water-wheel-driven ore stamp.

## Erzberg, Styria

Europe's largest open-pit iron mine, with a Hauly truck ride to the bottom of the working pit.

## Schaubergwerk Pyhrgas, Upper Austria

A graphite mine offering tours with caving gear.

## Terra Mystica & Terra Montana, Bad Bleiberg

A Carinthian lead-zinc mine with the only mine miniature railway still running in Austria.

## Salzwelten Altaussee

A Salzkammergut salt mine famous as the WWII Nazi-loot hiding place — and as one of Europe's quietest unter Tage spaces.

## Knappenwelt Gurgltal, Tarrenz

An open-air mining museum recreating Tyrolean silver-village life.

## Wolfsegg-Traunthaler Kohlewerks-Museum

Upper Austrian coal heritage, focused on lignite mining.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
