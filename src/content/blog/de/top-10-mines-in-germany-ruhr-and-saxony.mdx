Das deutsche Bergbau-Erbe stretches from the Ruhr coal basin in the west to the silver veins of the Saxon Erzgebirge — together a thousand years of European industrial history.

## Zeche Zollverein, Essen

The UNESCO-listed coal mine and coking plant, often called the most beautiful colliery in the world, anchors the Ruhr's post-industrial reinvention.

## Rammelsberg, Goslar

A thousand-year ore mine at the foot of the Harz mountains, UNESCO-inscribed in 1992 alongside the medieval town of Goslar.

## Freiberg Silver Mine, Saxony

The Reiche Zeche shaft, used by the world's oldest mining academy (TU Bergakademie Freiberg, 1765), offers descents into authentic Saxon silver workings.

## Salzbergwerk Berchtesgaden

A Bavarian salt mine in continuous operation since 1517; visitors slide down wooden chutes and cross an unter Tage brine lake by boat.

## Markus-Röhling-Stolln, Annaberg

An Erzgebirge silver gallery on the UNESCO Erzgebirge Mining Region list (inscribed 2019).

## Zeche Nachtigall, Witten

An early Ruhr drift mine on the river Ruhr, preserving the transition from coal-pit to deep mining.

## Kaiser-Wilhelm-Stollen, Clausthal-Zellerfeld

Upper Harz silver-and-lead galleries with a 19th-century waterwheel system that still functions.

## Salzbergwerk Bad Reichenhall

Bavarian Alpine salt works producing for two and a half millennia; the Alte Saline tour and brine ride are highlights.

## Asse and Schacht Konrad, Lower Saxony

Cold-war-era salt and iron mines repurposed as nuclear waste research sites — a sobering modern face of mining heritage.

## Schacht Reiche Zeche & Alte Elisabeth

Twin Freiberg shafts now used for teaching and visitor descents, with original 1849 steam engine still operable.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
