Coober Pedy in the South Australian desert produces 70 % of the world's gem-quality opal — and half its population lives unter Tage to escape 45 °C summer heat. It is one of the strangest towns on the planet.

## Entdeckung 1915

Opal was first found by 14-year-old Willie Hutchison on a gold-prospecting expedition led by his father. The name Coober Pedy comes from kupa piti, a Kokatha phrase often translated 'white man's hole'.

## The fields

The Coober Pedy opal field covers around 5000 km² and contains an estimated 1.5 million prospecting shafts. The opal occurs in nodules and seams within Cretaceous sandstone at 5–30 m depth.

## Dugouts

Around half the town's 1800 residents live in unter Tage 'dugouts' — homes excavated horizontally into the hillsides. Internal temperature is a constant 22 °C ganzjährig. The largest dugouts are bigger than typical suburban houses.

## Underground churches and hotels

The Serbian Orthodox Church, the Anglican Catacomb Church and several Catholic churches are all unter Tage. Two hotels — the Desert Cave and the Underground Motel — offer bedrooms cut into rock.

## Mining methods

Most extraction is small-scale: blower trucks suck up loosened rock from blast-loosened drives. The town hosts annual opal-festival competitions for the best find.

## Noodling

Visitors can 'noodle' — sift through tailings dumps — for free at marked public sites. Significant finds are routinely made by amateurs.

## The Big Winch and viewpoints

The Big Winch on a hill above town offers panoramic views of the moonscape. The Kanku-Breakaways Conservation Park, 30 km north, provides further desert spectacle.

## Praktische Hinweise

Coober Pedy lies 850 km north of Adelaide on the Stuart Highway. The Indian Pacific train stops at nearby Manguri. Carry water, fuel and patience for the heat.

## Cultural significance

Coober Pedy has hosted films from Mad Max Beyond Thunderdome to Pitch Black. Its mixed population includes residents born in over 40 countries — a function of the post-war opal-rush waves.
