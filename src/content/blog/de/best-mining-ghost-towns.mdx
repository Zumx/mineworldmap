When the ore ran out, the towns died. Ghost towns from the American west to Mexico, Chile and Australia preserve a bittersweet record of boom and collapse.

## Bodie, California

Preserved in 'arrested decay' by California State Parks. Population peaked at 10 000 in 1880; fewer than a dozen today.

## Calico, California

A silver town now restored as a county park.

## Garnet, Montana

A high-altitude gold-mining town preserved with original buildings.

## Sewell, Chile (UNESCO)

The 'city of stairs' above El Teniente — abandoned but inscribed.

## Real de Catorce, Mexico

A silver town in San Luis Potosí, partly revived as artists' colony.

## Pyramiden, Svalbard, Norway

A Soviet coal-mining settlement abandoned in 1998 with everything left in place.

## Goldfield, Nevada

Once Nevada's largest town; perhaps 250 people today.

## Burra, South Australia

Restored Cornish copper-mining town.

## Berlin, Nevada

A silver ghost town inside an ichthyosaur palaeontology state park.

## Kolmanskop, Namibia

A German diamond-mining town being swallowed by Namib dunes.
