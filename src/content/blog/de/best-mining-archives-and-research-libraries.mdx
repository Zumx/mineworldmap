Serious mining history depends on archives: company records, payroll books, mine plans, safety inspection reports. A handful of institutions hold the major collections.

## Cornish Mining Records Office, Redruth

The world's leading Cornish-tin and copper-mining archive.

## National Coal Mining Archive, Wakefield

British coal industry administrative records.

## Bergbau-Archiv, Bochum, Germany

The German mining archives — comprehensive Ruhr-coal records.

## Centre Historique des Archives du Travail, Roubaix

French industrial-relations archive including Nord-Pas-de-Calais coal.

## Hagley Museum and Library, Delaware, USA

Du Pont and broader American industrial archive including mining.

## Eveleth Mining Heritage Library, Minnesota

Mesabi range archives.

## LKAB Archives, Kiruna and Malmberget

Swedish iron-mining archives.

## Archive of the Royal School of Mines, Imperial College London

Institutional records and student collections back to 1851.

## Codelco Archives, Santiago, Chile

State copper company records back to 1971.

## Anglo-American Archive, Johannesburg

Critical Witwatersrand and Zambian-Copperbelt records.
