Salt mines are some of the most spectacular unter Tage spaces on the planet — vast halite cathedrals, brine lakes, and centuries of carved chapels.

## Wieliczka, Poland (UNESCO)

The world's most famous salt mine, in continuous extraction from the 13th century. The Chapel of St Kinga is carved entirely from rock salt.

## Bochnia Salt Mine, Poland

Older than Wieliczka (1248) but less visited; includes an unter Tage slide and overnight stays.

## Hallstatt, Austria

The world's oldest salt mine, in operation for 7000 years.

## Berchtesgaden, Bavaria

A working salt mine since 1517 with wooden slides and a brine-lake boat ride.

## Bad Reichenhall, Bavaria

Continuous salt production for 2500 years; the Alte Saline tour shows 19th-century pumps.

## Salina Turda, Romania

An unter Tage theme park 120 Metern below ground, with a Ferris wheel and lake.

## Praid, Romania

A working salt mine with an indoor playground for asthmatic children.

## Khewra, Pakistan

World's second-largest salt mine, with a small electric train and a mini-mosque carved from pink halite.

## Nemocón, Colombia

An Andean salt mine with mirror-lake reflective chambers.

## Dürrnberg, Salzburg, Austria

The Celtic salt mine that gave Salzburg its name; cross-border with Bavaria.

## Tourplanung

Salt mines are often warm and dry — pleasant ganzjährig and very accessible for older visitors. Die meisten Stätten verfügen über Besucherzentren open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
