Many historic mines used inclined-plane railways or cog tracks to move ore and men up impossible gradients. Several still run as visitor attractions.

## Mine de Sel de Bex, Switzerland

A 19th-century brine railway descending into the Vaudois Alps.

## Mina da Passagem, Brazil

The 120 m inclined-rail descent into the Mariana gold mine.

## Reichenbach Falls, Switzerland

Linked to historic mining funiculars in central Switzerland.

## Schwaz Silver Mine, Tyrol

An 800 m horizontal mining train into the Sigmund gallery.

## Kongsberg Silver Mines, Norway

The 2.3 km horizontal Christian Stoll train ride.

## Big Bog Mining Tour, Minnesota

A short cog descent at the Hibbing Hull-Rust mine.

## Cripple Creek and Victor, Colorado

A 4-foot-gauge narrow-gauge railroad through gold-rush mining country.

## White Pass and Yukon Route, Alaska/Canada

Built for the Klondike Gold Rush; still running as a tourist train.

## Quincy Mine, Michigan

An incline-railway descent into copper-country.

## Idrija Mercury Mine, Slovenia

A small mining-train ride to the Anthony's Shaft.
