Warm mine water, constant temperature, low-allergen air: a remarkable number of former mines have been repurposed as health-treatment centres.

## Salina Praid, Romania

Underground asthma sanatorium 120 m below ground.

## Wieliczka Health Resort, Poland

Respiratory sanatorium at 135 m depth in salt galleries.

## Bochnia Salt Mine Sanatorium, Poland

Older than Wieliczka, with overnight stays and treatment rooms.

## Khewra Salt Mine, Pakistan

An asthma-treatment clinic in pink halite chambers.

## Sulphur Springs at Tabernas, Spain

Mine-related warm-spring therapy in Andalucía.

## Bad Schlema, Germany

A radon-spa town on the Wismut uranium fields — radon was once seen as therapeutic.

## Jachymov, Czech Republic

The original radium-spa town, drawing on uranium-mine water.

## Wieliczka 'Health Resort Kinga'

The major Polish unter Tage sanatorium.

## Khewra Mosque chambers

Asthma treatment combined with religious tourism in Pakistan.

## Wettenberg Healing Mine, Germany

Smaller German radon-therapy mining site.
