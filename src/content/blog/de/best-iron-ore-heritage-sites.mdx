Iron ore powered every industrial revolution. These museums and working pits range from medieval bloomeries to the world's biggest open-pits.

## Kiruna, Sweden

LKAB's working iron mine, the world's largest unter Tage iron operation; visitor descents to 540 m.

## Erzberg, Styria, Austria

Europe's largest open-pit iron mine, with Hauly-truck tours.

## Rammelsberg, Goslar, Germany (UNESCO)

A thousand-year polymetallic ore mine, inscribed 1992.

## Iron Range, Minnesota, USA

Mesabi range with the Hibbing 'Hull Rust' pit viewing platform and several museums.

## Bilbao Mining Museum, Spain

Bizkaian iron heritage that fed the Basque steel industry.

## Falun (associated iron districts), Sweden

Bergslagen iron region in central Sweden, with the Engelsberg Ironworks (UNESCO).

## Carajás, Brazil

The world's largest single iron deposit, with limited visitor access.

## Ironbridge Gorge, England (UNESCO)

The first iron bridge (1779) and a complete industrial-revolution iron landscape.

## Big Bog and Hibbing, Minnesota

Open-pit observation and bus tours of working Mesabi iron operations.

## Sydvaranger, Kirkenes, Norway

Arctic-circle iron mine with surface visitor walks.

## Tourplanung

Iron heritage is often combined with steel — pair these sites with a smelter visit for the full chain. Die meisten Stätten verfügen über Besucherzentren open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
