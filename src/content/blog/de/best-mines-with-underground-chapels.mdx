Saint Barbara is the patron saint of miners. Some of the world's most extraordinary religious spaces are unter Tage chapels carved into salt, granite or coal galleries.

## Chapel of St Kinga, Wieliczka, Poland

The 101 m-deep salt chapel — the most famous of all.

## St Anthony's Chapel, Idrija, Slovenia

Carved into the entrance rock of the 1500 mining gallery.

## Sanctuary of Christ at Cerro Rico, Bolivia

Catholic chapel for working miners on the Potosí mountain.

## Chapel of Sta Barbara, Real del Monte, Mexico

A Cornish-built chapel in the central Mexican silver town.

## Salina Turda Chapel, Romania

A small Orthodox chapel inside the unter Tage theme park.

## Chapel of Sta Barbara, Banská Štiavnica, Slovakia

In the central Bartolomej adit.

## Khewra Mosque chambers, Pakistan

Salt-carved Islamic prayer rooms.

## St Barbara's Underground Mass, Big Pit, Wales

An annual December event in the colliery galleries.

## Serbian Orthodox Underground Church, Coober Pedy, Australia

Carved into opal-country sandstone.

## Catacomb Anglican Church, Coober Pedy

The English-tradition counterpart, deeper down.
