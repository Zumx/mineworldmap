Gold has driven empires, rushes and engineering feats. These heritage sites tell its story from Roman opencasts to nineteenth-century rushes — and several still let visitors swing a pan.

## Las Médulas, Spain (UNESCO)

Roman opencast workings in León province, where the ruina montium technique (deliberate hydraulic collapse) extracted tonnes of gold between AD 25 and 200; UNESCO-inscribed in 1997.

## Sutter's Mill, Coloma, California

Site of James W. Marshall's 1848 discovery that launched the California Gold Rush. The state park has a working replica of the sawmill.

## Sovereign Hill, Ballarat, Australia

A reconstructed 1850s gold-rush town in Victoria, with unter Tage mine tours and live panning.

## Klondike Goldfields, Yukon, Canada

Dawson City and Bonanza Creek preserve the 1896–1899 stampede; Parks Canada operates several site museums.

## Mina da Passagem, Brazil

A colonial gold mine in Mariana, Minas Gerais — visitor descent by inclined railway to flooded chambers.

## Ouro Preto, Brazil (UNESCO)

The capital of Portugal's American gold cycle; baroque churches and the Chico Rei and Encardideira mines.

## Kremnica, Slovakia

A medieval royal gold town that minted the Kremnica ducat for seven centuries.

## Roșia Montană, Romania (UNESCO)

Roman gold workings inscribed in 2021 after a long civic battle against modern reopening.

## Gold Reef City, Johannesburg, South Africa

A 1880s Witwatersrand gold mine turned theme park and museum.

## Verespatak / Roșia Poieni, Romania

Adjacent to the UNESCO site, an active copper-and-gold mine demonstrating the modern industry's scale.

## Tourplanung

If you visit only one, make it either Las Médulas for ancient awe or Sovereign Hill for hands-on rush nostalgia. Die meisten Stätten verfügen über Besucherzentren open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
