Das portugiesische Bergbau-Erbe runs from Roman gold to 20th-century tungsten and pyrite — a rich and underrated Iberian story.

## Minas de São Domingos

A Roman-era copper-pyrite mine that operated until 1966 under a British concession; a striking abandoned post-industrial landscape in the Alentejo.

## Aljustrel Mine

An Iberian Pyrite Belt mine, recently reopened for zinc; museum on three thousand years of extraction.

## Panasqueira Tungsten Mine, Beira Baixa

Europe's only major working tungsten mine and a sometime visitor site; tungsten from here armed both sides of WWII.

## Lousal Mine and Ciência Viva Centre

A pyrite mine that became a science-and-history museum after closure in 1988.

## Couto Mineiro do Pejão

A Douro-valley coal mine closed in 1994, now a heritage trail.

## Minas da Borralha

A tungsten field in the northern mountains, with extensive abandoned village ruins.

## Mina de Sal-Gema de Loulé

An Algarve rock-salt mine, the only one in Portugal, with annual visitor tours.

## Minas do Lousal Boat Tour

A flooded gallery navigated by small boat — a rare experience in Iberia.

## Geopark Naturtejo

A UNESCO-listed geopark including several abandoned tin and gold workings.

## Roman Gold Workings of Tresminas

Trás-os-Montes; spectacular Roman opencast gold workings on the scale of Las Médulas in Spain.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
