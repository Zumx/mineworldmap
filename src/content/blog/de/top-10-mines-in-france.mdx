Das französische Bergbau-Erbe runs from the coal basins of Nord-Pas-de-Calais and Lorraine to the silver of the Cévennes and the iron of the Vosges — a thousand-year industrial archaeology.

## Centre Historique Minier de Lewarde

France's largest mining museum, on the former Fosse Delloye, in the UNESCO-listed Nord-Pas-de-Calais coalfield.

## Carreau Wendel, Petite-Rosselle

A vast Lorraine coal museum with a reconstructed unter Tage gallery on the German border.

## Mines d'Argent des Rois Francs, Melle

Silver-and-lead workings of the early Frankish kings, used to mint the deniers that financed Charlemagne's empire.

## Musée de la Mine, Saint-Étienne

The Couriot pithead frame and miners' lamp room on the site that defined the French coal industry.

## Tellure, Sainte-Marie-aux-Mines

Alsatian silver mines from the 16th century, with a 600-metre unter Tage train ride.

## La Mine Bleue, Anjou

A unique blue-slate mine in western France, with a train descent and an exhibition on roofing slate.

## Musée de la Mine, La Machine (Nièvre)

A central French coal museum on a mining estate that operated from 1689 to 1974.

## Mines de Bruoux, Provence

Ochre mines in Provence with high cathedral-like unter Tage galleries colored deep red and yellow.

## Bassin Minier Nord-Pas-de-Calais

The whole 120-kilometre coalfield was UNESCO-inscribed in 2012, including 51 spoil tips, 21 pit headframes and four workers' housing estates.

## Mine Image, La Motte-d'Aveillans

An Isère coal mine where visitors ride a real mine train into an authentic gallery used until 1997.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
