Die wallonischen Kohlebecken Belgiens powered the first industrial revolution on continental Europe — and four pithead sites are now UNESCO-inscribed as the Major Mining Sites of Wallonia.

## Bois du Cazier, Marcinelle (UNESCO)

A coal mine that closed after the 1956 disaster in which 262 miners died — most of them Italian guest workers; today a moving museum and Italian-Belgian memorial.

## Grand-Hornu (UNESCO)

A neo-classical industrial complex of the early 19th century, designed as an entire workers' town around the mine.

## Le Bois-du-Luc (UNESCO)

One of the world's oldest continuously operating mining companies (1685); a complete preserved village.

## Blegny-Mine (UNESCO)

The last coal mine to close in Belgium (1980), with an unter Tage tour and an authentic pit-bath.

## C-Mine, Genk

A Flemish coal mine reborn as a creative campus with theatre, art schools and an unter Tage experience walk.

## Mijnmuseum Beringen

The largest preserved mining heritage in Flanders, with the iconic twin headframes.

## Domaine de Mariemont

A royal estate with a museum on industrial-revolution Belgian history including coal.

## Espace Arthur Masson

A coal-and-quarry-themed open-air museum in the Ardennes.

## Marble Quarries of Rance

Belgium's historic red-marble quarrying region; a separate but related stone-extraction tradition.

## Trooz Bricks and Coal Trail

A Liège-area mining-and-brickworks heritage path.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
