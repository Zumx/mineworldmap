Wieliczka, near Kraków, is the most famous salt mine in the world. Worked continuously from the 13th century until 1996, it produced the rock salt that funded medieval Poland and still draws over a million visitors a year.

## Geschichte

Salt extraction at Wieliczka began in the late 1200s under Casimir I. By the 14th century the royal Żupy Krakowskie produced a third of crown revenue. Mining continued for seven centuries before commercial extraction ceased in 1996; salt is still produced today as a tourism by-product.

## Chapel of Saint Kinga

The mine's most famous chamber is the unter Tage Chapel of St Kinga, 101 m below the surface and entirely carved from rock salt — including the chandeliers, made of salt crystals dissolved and re-grown for clarity. Daily mass is still celebrated here.

## The tourist route

The standard 3.5 km route descends 800 wooden steps to 135 m below ground and includes around twenty chambers, including the Crystal Caves and the Erazm Barącz Chamber with its brine lake. The full tour takes two hours.

## The miners' route

A more adventurous option, with overalls and helmet lamps, takes visitors into less-finished galleries and ends with a working winder ride to the surface.

## Health applications

The 'Health Resort Kinga' sanatorium operates at 135 m below ground and treats respiratory illnesses with the salt-saturated unter Tage air.

## UNESCO-Eintrag

Wieliczka and the nearby Bochnia Salt Mine were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1978, in the very first batch.

## Praktische Hinweise

Temperature is a steady 14 °C, the air is dry, and even small children manage the walk well. Pre-booking is essential in summer; queues at the door reach two hours.

## Nearby Bochnia

Bochnia, 30 km east, is older than Wieliczka (1248) and far less visited — it offers an unter Tage slide, multimedia ride and even bedrooms for the night.

## Cultural impact

The mine has appeared in films from the silent era onwards and is celebrated in Polish literature; Adam Mickiewicz, Goethe and Chopin all descended.
