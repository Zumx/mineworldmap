Peru gehört zu den großen Bergbaunationen der Welt — silver, copper, gold, zinc — and its mining heritage stretches from pre-Inca times through Spanish colonial wealth to today's open pits.

## Cerro de Pasco

A working open-pit polymetallic mine that swallowed a town of 70 000 people; one of the most extreme mining landscapes in the world.

## Hualgayoc Silver District

Colonial silver workings in northern Peru, with hundreds of small adits dotting the puna.

## Yanacocha Gold Mine, Cajamarca

South America's largest gold mine, with a small interpretation centre at its perimeter.

## Casapalca Silver Mine

A working high-altitude silver mine that occasionally opens visitor tours.

## Quiruvilca Polymetallic Mine

La Libertad district; closed and reopened several times, now a partial museum.

## Toquepala Copper Mine, Tacna

A 1959-era open-pit copper mine with a public viewing platform — the deepest pit in Peru.

## La Oroya Smelter Town

A heavily polluted but historically important polymetallic smelter site with industrial-heritage interest.

## Huancavelica Mercury Mine (Santa Bárbara)

The colonial mercury supply for Potosí silver — UNESCO-listed since 2024.

## Madre de Dios Gold Trail

Modern artisanal-gold tourism in the Amazonian basin; a difficult ethical destination.

## National Geology and Mining Museum, Lima

A serious museum maintained by INGEMMET in San Borja.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
