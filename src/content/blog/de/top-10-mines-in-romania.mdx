Das rumänische Bergbau-Erbe runs from the Roman gold of the Apuseni to the salt of Transylvania — and one of the most spectacular unter Tage attractions in Europe.

## Salina Turda

A medieval salt mine reopened in 2010 as a vast unter Tage theme park: a Ferris wheel, a lake with rowing boats and an amphitheatre, all 120 Metern unter Tage.

## Roșia Montană (UNESCO 2021)

Roman-era gold workings in the Apuseni mountains, inscribed by UNESCO in 2021 after a long civil-society campaign against a modern cyanide-leaching project.

## Praid Salt Mine, Harghita

A working salt mine since Roman times; the visitor section includes a chapel, a playground and an asthma-treatment centre.

## Slănic Prahova Salt Mine

A vast halite cathedral with 50-metre-high carved pillars.

## Cavnic Mining Museum

Maramureș base-metal heritage with original timber headframes.

## Petrila Coal Mine, Jiu Valley

Closed in 2015 after a century and a half, now a contested cultural-regeneration project led by cartoonist Ion Barbu.

## Brad Gold Museum

An Apuseni gold museum displaying native gold of extraordinary crystalline quality.

## Râșca and Crucea Uranium Mines

Former communist-era uranium workings in Bukovina, with a small but candid memorial museum.

## Ocnele Mari Salt Mine

A working salt mine with a deep visitor section dating to the 18th century.

## Baia Mare Mineralogy Museum

Northern Transylvania's finest mineral collection, with type specimens of several new minerals first described here.

## Routenplanung

Diese zehn Stätten umfassen sehr unterschiedliche Klimazonen, languages and accessibility levels. Gruppieren Sie sie nach Region, allow einen halben Tag for each unter Tage descent, and be ready for kühle Temperaturen (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Die örtlichen Führer sind häufig pensionierte Bergleute — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
