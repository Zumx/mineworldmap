A handful of mines preserve flooded galleries that can be navigated by boat — small punts, brine-lake gondolas or even rail-mounted electric craft. These are among the most unusual unter Tage experiences anywhere.

## Bonne Espérance, Belgium

Flooded lead-zinc gallery near Liège, navigated by electric punt.

## Wieliczka Salt Mine, Poland

The Erazm Barącz Chamber brine lake, crossed by gondola.

## Salzwelten Dürrnberg, Austria

Brine lake on the Salzburg salt mine tour, crossed by raft.

## Mine Bleue, Anjou, France

Train descent then a small boat through blue-slate flooded workings.

## Wookey Hole, Somerset, England

More cave than mine, but with adjacent ochre workings.

## Speedwell Cavern, Castleton, England

An 18th-century lead mine navigated by boat through a 500 m flooded gallery.

## Bonne Mine d'Hozémont, Belgium

Another flooded Walloon gallery accessible by punt.

## Mina La Jayona, Spain

Iron mine in Extremadura with a flooded section.

## Olavsgruva, Røros, Norway

Acoustic concerts in a flooded chamber 50 m down.

## Pioneer Mine, Iron Mountain, USA

Flooded Michigan iron mine with electric-launch tours.
