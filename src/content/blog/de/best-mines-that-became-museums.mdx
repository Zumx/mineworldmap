Some of the world's finest museums are former working mines — preserving headframes, galleries and machinery on the actual ground where they were used.

## Big Pit, Blaenavon, Wales

Closed 1980, museum 1983. Free entry, ex-miner guides, 90 m descent.

## Zollverein, Essen, Germany

Closed 1986, UNESCO 2001. The Bauhaus colliery turned cultural campus.

## Lewarde, France

Closed 1971, museum 1984. The largest mining museum in France.

## Idrija, Slovenia

Closed 1995, UNESCO 2012. A complete town-and-mine ensemble.

## Wieliczka, Poland

Active extraction ended 1996, museum since 1722 in some form. The salt cathedral.

## Falun, Sweden

Closed 1992, UNESCO 2001. The Great Pit.

## Rammelsberg, Germany

Closed 1988, UNESCO 1992. A thousand-year museum.

## Kongsberg, Norway

Closed 1958, museum since same year. Royal silver.

## Beckley Exhibition Coal Mine, USA

Mine-car tours of Appalachian bituminous.

## Cottesloe Big Pit, Australia

Reconstructed gold-rush working pit at Sovereign Hill.
