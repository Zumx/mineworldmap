Germany's mining history runs a thousand years deep, from medieval Harz silver
to the coal that powered the Ruhr. It has converted that legacy into some of
the finest mining museums and show mines in the world.

## 1. Zeche Zollverein, Essen

A UNESCO World Heritage coal mine and coking plant, the icon of Ruhr industrial
culture and a stunning piece of Bauhaus-influenced design.

## 2. Rammelsberg, Goslar

A UNESCO mine worked continuously for over a thousand years, with original
machinery and underground tours in the Harz.

## 3. Erzbergwerk Rammelsberg / Samson Pit, Sankt Andreasberg

Home to a working 19th-century man-engine, a rare surviving piece of mining
technology, still demonstrated.

## 4. Markus-Röhling-Stollen, Ore Mountains

A show mine with an underground mine train in the UNESCO Erzgebirge mining
region.

## 5. Reiche Zeche, Freiberg

A research and teaching mine of the world's oldest mining university,
descendable on guided tours.

## 6. Altenberg tin mine, Ore Mountains

A vast collapse crater (the Pinge) and tin-mining museum in Saxony's
UNESCO landscape.

## 7. Kupferschiefer / Mansfeld, Saxony-Anhalt

Heartland of historic copper-shale mining with preserved shafts and museum
collections.

## 8. Auswandererhaus — note: Ramsbeck, Sauerland

A lead and zinc show mine entered by mine railway deep into the mountain.

## 9. Nightingale (Nachtigall) Colliery, Witten

One of the oldest preserved coal mines in the Ruhr, telling the story of where
deep mining began.

## 10. Glückauf-Bergwerk / Black Forest silver mines, e.g. Schauinsland

Freiburg-area medieval silver mines with extensive underground tours.

## How German show mines work

Many are *Besucherbergwerke* — purpose-organised visitor mines — with guided
descents by foot, cage, or mine railway, often run by heritage associations.
The UNESCO sites (Zollverein, Rammelsberg, the Erzgebirge) combine museum,
landscape, and underground tours. Book ahead; underground tours have capacity
limits.

## Timing and conditions

Underground temperatures sit around 8–12°C all year — bring warm clothing
regardless of season. Many smaller show mines run reduced winter schedules;
the big UNESCO sites are year-round. The Erzgebirge mining region is best
combined into a multi-site route.

## Safety and respect

Follow the guide exactly, wear issued safety gear, and never explore old
workings alone. These mines memorialise extremely hard and often deadly labour;
the museums take that seriously and visitors should too. No specimen collecting.
