The Kirunavaara iron deposit in Swedish Lapland produces high-grade magnetite ore that has fed European steel for over a century. The mine is now so vast that the city above it is being physically moved.

## Geologia

Kirunavaara is a massive apatite-magnetite body — the largest deposit of its type in the world. Iron grade averages 60 %, exceptionally high for a major deposit.

## Storia

Industrial extraction began in 1900 under the Luossavaara-Kiirunavaara AB (LKAB) company. The Malmbanan railway to Narvik on the Norwegian coast opened the same year, allowing tutto l'anno ice-free export.

## The 1903 city

The city of Kiruna was planned in 1900 by the architect Per Hallman as a model company town. The kyrka (1912) is one of Sweden's most beloved wooden churches.

## Operations

LKAB extracts around 28 million tonnes of ore a year by sublevel caving from the main haulage level at 1365 m below surface. New extraction levels are being prepared at 1565 m.

## The moving city

Subsidence from the deepening mine threatens the historic city centre. From 2014 LKAB has financed the relocation of 6000 inhabitants and major buildings — including the church, lifted in 2025 and moved 3 km on rails — to a new town centre 3 km east. It is one of the largest urban-relocation projects in modern history.

## Visitor experience

LKAB runs the InfoMine tourist mine 540 m below ground, with bus access by a 5-km spiral road. The tour covers extraction technology, refining and the relocation project.

## Sápmi context

Kiruna sits in Sámi reindeer-herding territory. The relationship between the mine, the Swedish state and the Sámi communities is a continuing political question.

## Other Kiruna attractions

The ICEHOTEL at Jukkasjärvi (50 km east), the Esrange space centre and the Abisko aurora reserve all sit within a day-trip of Kiruna.

## Consigli pratici

Kiruna is 145 km north of the Arctic Circle. Polar nights last from mid-December to early January; the midnight sun from late May to mid-July.
