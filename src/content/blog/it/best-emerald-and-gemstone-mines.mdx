Coloured gemstones come from a handful of places, often deeply remote. A few of those places welcome visitors — and some let you keep what you find.

## Muzo and Chivor, Colombia

Source of the world's finest emeralds since pre-Columbian times; small-group tours operate from Bogotá.

## Mogok, Myanmar

The 'Valley of Rubies' — a controlled-access region producing the world's best pigeon-blood rubies.

## Ratnapura, Sri Lanka

Gem city for sapphire, ruby and many other stones; visitor gem-pits and a national museum.

## Crater of Diamonds, Arkansas

The only diamond field open to the public for keep-what-you-find digging.

## Spencer, Idaho

Fee-mining for precious opal in the American west.

## Herkimer Diamond Mines, New York

Double-terminated quartz crystals — 'Herkimer diamonds' — open for visitor extraction.

## Wegel Tena, Welo, Ethiopia

The world's main hydrophane-opal field since 2008; specialist permits required.

## Anakie Sapphire Gemfields, Queensland

Open fossicking grounds with public-access digging.

## Brazilian Minas Gerais Gemstone Belt

Aquamarine, tourmaline and topaz fields with the Teófilo Otoni gem market as the centre.

## Cowee Valley, North Carolina

Ruby-and-sapphire fee-mining streams with family-friendly operations.

## Come pianificare la visita

Coloured-gem extraction is often artisanal and informal — buy and visit through ethical operators. La maggior parte dei siti dispone di centri visitatori open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
