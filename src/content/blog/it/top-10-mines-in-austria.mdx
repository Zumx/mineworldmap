Il patrimonio alpino di sale e argento dell'Austria and silver heritage dates back to the Iron Age Hallstatt culture — the world's first major industrial mining tradition — and continues today as living tourism.

## Salzwelten Hallstatt

The world's oldest salt mine, in continuous operation for over 7000 years; the prehistoric tour includes a 64-metre miners' slide.

## Salzwelten Salzburg (Dürrnberg)

A Celtic and medieval salt mine where visitors cross an sotterraneo brine lake by boat.

## Schwaz Silver Mine, Tyrol

Once the largest silver mine in 16th-century Europe; train descent into 800 metres of horizontal gallery.

## Schaubergwerk Mauterndorf

A small Salzburg silver mine with a preserved water-wheel-driven ore stamp.

## Erzberg, Styria

Europe's largest open-pit iron mine, with a Hauly truck ride to the bottom of the working pit.

## Schaubergwerk Pyhrgas, Upper Austria

A graphite mine offering tours with caving gear.

## Terra Mystica & Terra Montana, Bad Bleiberg

A Carinthian lead-zinc mine with the only mine miniature railway still running in Austria.

## Salzwelten Altaussee

A Salzkammergut salt mine famous as the WWII Nazi-loot hiding place — and as one of Europe's quietest sotterraneo spaces.

## Knappenwelt Gurgltal, Tarrenz

An open-air mining museum recreating Tyrolean silver-village life.

## Wolfsegg-Traunthaler Kohlewerks-Museum

Upper Austrian coal heritage, focused on lignite mining.

## Pianificare l'itinerario

These ten sites span very different climates, languages and accessibility levels. Group them by region, allow a full half-day for each sotterraneo descent, and be ready for temperature fresche (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Local guides are often ex minatori — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
