Lead and zinc are often the silent partners of silver mining. Their heritage sites range from Roman galleries to vast Mississippi-Valley open pits.

## Killhope, North Pennines, England

A restored Victorian lead mine with a working water-wheel.

## Wanlockhead, Scotland

Scotland's highest village; lead-mining since the 16th century with the Loch Nell Mine open to visitors.

## Pribram, Czech Republic

A great Bohemian silver-lead-uranium district with extensive sotterraneo tour.

## Bleiberg, Carinthia, Austria

Terra Mystica & Terra Montana mines tell the lead-zinc story of the Bleiberg district.

## Cerro de Pasco, Peru

Working open-pit polymetallic mine; large-scale lead and zinc producer with historical museum.

## Joplin and Picher, Missouri/Oklahoma, USA

Tri-State district, once the world's largest lead-zinc field; difficult heritage of contamination.

## Bawdwin, Myanmar

Historic lead-silver-zinc mine with British colonial machinery and ongoing operations.

## Mount Isa, Queensland, Australia

Working lead-zinc-copper-silver mine with public mine-tour and museum.

## Tara Mine, Ireland

Europe's largest zinc mine; limited but possible visitor tours.

## Reocín Mine, Cantabria, Spain

Historic Iberian zinc producer with a small heritage museum.

## Come pianificare la visita

Lead heritage is often delicate ground — sites usually combine industrial history with environmental remediation stories. La maggior parte dei siti dispone di centri visitatori open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
