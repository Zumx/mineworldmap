What happens to a mine when it closes? From the Ruhr's post-industrial parks to the Welsh re-greening of slate spoil, mine remediation is now a vast global engineering and ecological discipline.

## The scale of the problem

There are estimated 1 million abandoned mines worldwide. In the US alone, the Abandoned Mine Lands Programme has documented over 500 000 sites; in the UK, the Coal Authority manages 174 000 disused shafts.

## Acid mine drainage

The dominant chemical legacy. When sulphide ores meet air and water, they generate sulphuric acid that mobilises heavy metals — copper, zinc, cadmium, arsenic — into watercourses. Treatment is expensive, often perpetual.

## Land regrading

Open-pit mines are increasingly required to be back-filled or re-profiled. The 1977 Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act in the US established the principle of 'restoration to approximate original contour' for coal.

## Re-vegetation

Spoil tips, tailings dams and waste rock are stabilised with engineered topsoil and pioneer species. Welsh slate tips have been recolonised with heather and crowberry; Pennsylvania anthracite tips with hybrid grasses and pines.

## The Ruhr example

The IBA Emscher Park (1989–1999) was the planning framework that turned the Ruhr's derelict industrial sites into parks, museums and housing. Landschaftspark Duisburg-Nord (a blast furnace landscape) and Nordsternpark in Gelsenkirchen are exemplars.

## Welsh slate

The Slate Landscape of North-West Wales was UNESCO-inscribed in 2021. Vast spoil tips are now part of the protected landscape, slowly being recolonised by mosses and lichens.

## Tailings dam safety

Brumadinho (Brazil, 2019) and Mount Polley (Canada, 2014) failures killed hundreds and ruined river systems. The Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management (2020) was the result.

## Rewilding

Some abandoned mines have become ecologically valuable: bat caves, peregrine cliffs, otter rivers, orchid-rich grasslands on lime tips. Cornwall's tin-spoil heaths host nationally rare invertebrates.

## Heritage versus restoration

The conservation tension between preserving industrial structures and erasing the mine is genuine and unresolved. UNESCO inscription often arbitrates — Blaenavon, Zollverein and Cornwall represent the compromise.
