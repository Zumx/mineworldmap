Some open-pit mines are so vast they are visible from space — and most large ones have dedicated viewing platforms for visitors who want to see industrial scale in real life.

## Bingham Canyon, Utah, USA

1.2 km deep, 4 km across; visitor centre and viewing terrace.

## Chuquicamata, Chile

A 1 km-deep open pit, viewed from a perimeter platform.

## Erzberg, Styria, Austria

Europe's largest iron mine with Hauly-truck access to the pit floor.

## Hull Rust Mahoning, Hibbing, Minnesota

America's first Mesabi range open pit, with a public viewpoint.

## Mirny Open Pit, Russia

525 m-deep diamond pit in Yakutia; perimeter view.

## Kimberley Big Hole, South Africa

Hand-dug 215 m pit with a glass-floor viewing platform.

## Super Pit, Kalgoorlie, Australia

A 3.5 km-long, 1.5 km-wide gold pit with a Hannans North observation deck.

## Toquepala, Peru

Public viewing platform over the Tacna copper mine.

## Fushun West Pit, China

Asia's largest historic open-pit coal mine, with a museum and viewpoint.

## El Teniente / Sewell, Chile

Mine-perimeter viewpoint adjoining the UNESCO town.
