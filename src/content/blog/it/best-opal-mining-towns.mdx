Opal is mined in a handful of remote places. The towns built around the workings are eccentric, deeply sotterraneo, and unforgettable.

## Coober Pedy, South Australia

Half the population lives sotterraneo in 'dugouts' to escape 45 °C heat; the world's largest source of precious opal.

## Lightning Ridge, New South Wales

The only commercial source of black opal; tours include the 'big opal' open-cuts.

## White Cliffs, New South Wales

A small opal town with sotterraneo hotels and a solar-power-station combination.

## Andamooka, South Australia

An older opal field with field-noodling permits for visitors.

## Mintabie, South Australia

Closed as a community in 2020 but landscape and tailings remain visitable.

## Quilpie, Queensland

Boulder-opal town with a public mine and gallery.

## Yowah, Queensland

Famed for the 'Yowah nut' opal nodules; an annual festival.

## Wegel Tena, Welo, Ethiopia

The world's leading source of hydrophane opal since 2008; specialist tours possible.

## Quéretaro, Mexico

Fire-opal mines accessible from Mexico City with day-tour operators.

## Spencer, Idaho, USA

Fee-mining opal site in the American west.

## Come pianificare la visita

Opal towns can be reached only by long drives — plan fuel, water and at least two nights on site. La maggior parte dei siti dispone di centri visitatori open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
