L'Indonesia è una delle maggiori nazioni minerarie del mondo — copper, gold, nickel, tin, coal — and its colonial and contemporary heritage is sprinkled across the archipelago.

## Sawahlunto (UNESCO)

A Dutch colonial coal-mining town in West Sumatra, UNESCO-inscribed in 2019 for its 19th-century mining landscape, narrow-gauge railway and Lubang Mbah Suro tunnel.

## Grasberg Mine, Papua

Freeport-McMoRan's vast copper-and-gold complex, one of the largest in the world; access is restricted but the scale defines modern Indonesian mining.

## Bangka and Belitung Tin Islands

Centuries of tin extraction with surviving Chinese-Hakka mining heritage and museum.

## Pongkor Gold Mine, West Java

A working sotterraneo gold mine that occasionally receives visitor groups; near Bogor.

## Ombilin Coal Mining, Sawahlunto

The historic 1891 colliery at the heart of the Sawahlunto UNESCO inscription, with a museum.

## Cikotok Gold Mine, Banten

A Dutch-colonial gold mine, the first such operation in the Dutch East Indies.

## Bukit Asam Coal Heritage, South Sumatra

Working steam locomotives still haul coal from this 1919 colliery.

## Soroako Nickel Mine, Sulawesi

Vale's flagship Indonesian nickel project; tourism is limited.

## Martabe Gold Mine, North Sumatra

A modern open-pit gold mine with educational visitor programmes.

## Geological Museum of Bandung

A serious Dutch-founded mineral and mining museum with national coverage.

## Pianificare l'itinerario

These ten sites span very different climates, languages and accessibility levels. Group them by region, allow a full half-day for each sotterraneo descent, and be ready for temperature fresche (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Local guides are often ex minatori — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
