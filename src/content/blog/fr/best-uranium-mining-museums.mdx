Uranium has a uniquely fraught heritage: Cold-war labour, environmental contamination and a complex modern legacy. A handful of sites tell the story candidly.

## Jáchymov, Czech Republic

The world's first uranium-mining town; Marie Curie's radium came from here. The Old Mint museum covers both the silver and uranium phases.

## Wismut Museum, Bad Schlema, Germany

The Soviet-East German uranium project Wismut produced 230 000 tonnes of uranium between 1946 and 1990 — Europe's biggest secret industry.

## Grand Junction Atomic Legacy Cabin, Colorado

A Department of Energy interpretive site on Colorado Plateau uranium history.

## Atomic Testing Museum, Las Vegas

Wider Cold-war legacy including uranium-supply chain.

## Élliot Lake Nuclear and Mining Museum, Ontario

Centred on Canada's largest uranium-mining boom and bust.

## Rößler Schaubergwerk, Schlema

Surface uranium-heritage walk in the German Erzgebirge.

## Ronneburg Uranium Heritage Trail, Thuringia

Former Wismut open-pits reclaimed and trail-marked after a billion-euro clean-up.

## Râșca Uranium Mine, Bukovina, Romania

A small but candid Communist-era memorial museum.

## Olympic Dam Visitor Centre, South Australia

Working copper-uranium mine with public information centre.

## Port Radium / Sahtu, NWT, Canada

The Dene-community-led memorial on the Eldorado uranium supply for the Manhattan Project.
