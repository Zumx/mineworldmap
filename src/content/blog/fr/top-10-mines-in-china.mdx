Le patrimoine minier chinois stretches across millennia — from Bronze-Age copper workings to the great Tongling smelters and the modern coal cities of Shanxi.

## Tonglushan Ancient Copper Mine, Hubei

A Bronze-Age copper mine, in use from 1200 BC, with the world's earliest preserved deep-shaft workings — now a museum.

## Yangshuo–Pingshan Tin Mine, Yunnan

Historic Yunnanese tin workings, with the city of Gejiu as its centre.

## Datong Coal Mining Cultural Park, Shanxi

A converted Datong colliery with souterrain tours and a coal-civilisation museum.

## Fushun West Open-Pit Mine, Liaoning

Once the largest open-pit coal mine in Asia; now closed and partially turned into a viewing platform.

## Kaiping Coal Mine Heritage, Hebei

Tangshan's 1878 coal mine — the first modern coal mine in China — now a museum.

## Dexing Copper Mine, Jiangxi

A working open-pit copper mine; the largest in China, with a public observation deck.

## Wanshan Mercury Mine, Guizhou

The historic 'mercury capital of China'; closed in 2002 and now a national mining park.

## Anshan Iron Mine, Liaoning

Historic iron heart of north-eastern China, with a steel-and-mining museum.

## Jinchang Nickel and Copper Mine, Gansu

A modern mega-mine with limited visitor access; the city was built around it.

## Tongling National Mine Park, Anhui

Twenty-six centuries of copper mining in one preserved landscape.

## Planifier l'itinéraire

These ten sites span very different climates, languages and accessibility levels. Group them by region, allow a full half-day for each souterrain descent, and be ready for des températures fraîches (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Local guides are often anciens mineurs — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
