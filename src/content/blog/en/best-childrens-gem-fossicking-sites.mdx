Fee-mining and fossicking sites let children dig, sift and keep what they find. They are among the best family-friendly entries into mining heritage.

## Crater of Diamonds State Park, Arkansas

The only diamond field in the world open for keep-what-you-find digging.

## Spencer Opal Mines, Idaho

Family fee-mining for precious opal in the American west.

## Anakie Sapphire Gemfields, Queensland, Australia

Public fossicking grounds with hire equipment.

## Herkimer Diamond Mines, New York

Double-terminated quartz crystals open for amateur extraction.

## Cowee Valley, North Carolina

Ruby-and-sapphire fee-mining streams.

## Wegel Tena Gem Walks, Ethiopia

Specialist guided opal walks in northern Ethiopia.

## Saint-Cybardeaux, France

Trilobite fossil-collecting in old quarries.

## Coober Pedy Public Noodling, Australia

Free opal-sifting at marked public sites.

## Cherokee Ruby Mine, North Carolina

Family-oriented ruby fee-mining.

## Diamond Hill, Antreville, South Carolina

Quartz-crystal fee-mining.
