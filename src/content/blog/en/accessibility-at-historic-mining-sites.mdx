Mines are intrinsically challenging environments for visitors with limited mobility. A growing number of sites now offer accessible tours, virtual reality alternatives and surface-only itineraries.

## Wieliczka 'Tourist Route' accessibility

A modified, partially wheelchair-accessible route covers 1.8 km of the standard tour.

## Big Pit surface tour

Full wheelchair access to surface galleries; underground requires the cage and is restricted.

## Zollverein

Most surface buildings, the Red Dot Museum and the Ruhr Museum are fully accessible. Some galleries have stairs.

## Idrija museum sections

The town museum is fully accessible; the underground gallery is not.

## Rammelsberg surface route

Wheelchair-accessible surface circuit covers the dressing plant and several major galleries.

## Falun museum and Great Pit viewpoint

Both fully wheelchair-accessible; the underground is not.

## Bingham Canyon

Fully accessible visitor centre and viewing terrace; no underground option.

## Beamish

Partial; the colliery village has gravel surfaces but the museum runs a free buggy service.

## Lewarde

The dressing plant and surface museum are accessible; the gallery descent is not.

## Virtual reality alternatives

Wieliczka, Zollverein and Big Pit all now offer VR alternatives to the underground tour for visitors who cannot make the descent.
