Coal built modernity and broke generations of bodies. These museums tell the story with seriousness and depth, from Wales to Pennsylvania to the Ruhr.

## Big Pit, Blaenavon, Wales (UNESCO)

A free-entry national museum where former miners lead 90-minute descents to a 90 m gallery.

## Zeche Zollverein, Essen (UNESCO)

Shaft XII, often called the most beautiful coal mine in the world, with the Ruhr Museum in the former washery.

## National Coal Mining Museum for England, Wakefield

Genuine underground tours led by retired Yorkshire miners.

## Centre Historique Minier de Lewarde, France

The largest mining museum in France, on a closed Fosse Delloye.

## Lackawanna Coal Mine, Pennsylvania, USA

Mine-car descent into a Scranton anthracite seam.

## Beamish Open Air Museum, County Durham

A living-history museum with a working drift mine.

## Beckley Exhibition Coal Mine, West Virginia

Mine-car tour of a real Appalachian seam, with former miner guides.

## Bois du Cazier, Marcinelle, Belgium (UNESCO)

Memorial-museum on the 1956 disaster that killed 262 mostly Italian miners.

## Kentucky Coal Mining Museum, Benham

Eastern Kentucky coal heritage in a 1920s company town.

## Bergbaumuseum Bochum, Germany

Largest mining museum in the world by floor area, with a 17-tonne shaft tower (Förderturm) marker.

## How to plan a tour

Most of these sites cluster well: Wales, the Ruhr and Appalachia are each one-day mining trails in their own right. Most sites have visitor centres open from spring to autumn and offer guided descents in English in summer.
