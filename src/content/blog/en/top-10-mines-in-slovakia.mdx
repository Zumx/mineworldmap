Slovakia's central mountains hold some of Europe's most important medieval mining towns — Banská Štiavnica above all — together with thermal spas built on former mine waters.

## Banská Štiavnica (UNESCO)

A UNESCO-inscribed medieval silver town that produced a tenth of Europe's silver in the 18th century and hosted the world's first mining academy in 1762.

## Old Castle and Mining Museum, Banská Štiavnica

Exhibits on the Tajov silver veins and the Hauerland German-speaking miners' settlements.

## Bartolomej Adit, Banská Štiavnica

A long horizontal gallery accessible on foot with original 18th-century rail tracks.

## Banská Bystrica Copper Museum

Copper extraction in the kingdom of Hungary, with displays on the Thurzó-Fugger company that supplied Renaissance Europe.

## Kremnica Gold Mine

A medieval royal gold mine that operated for seven centuries and supplied the Kremnica ducat, one of Europe's most stable gold coins.

## Štítnik and Rožňava Iron Mines

An ore region in the Slovak Karst, with several preserved 19th-century smelters.

## Špania Dolina Copper Mines

A picturesque mining village built around the copper veins worked from the 13th century.

## Pezinok Gold Mine

A small-scale gold field near Bratislava, now reopened as a museum with safety descents.

## Slovenský Geologický Múzeum, Bratislava

National geology museum with a comprehensive Slovak mining exhibit.

## Solivar Salt Works, Prešov

A 17th-century salt extraction site with original wooden machinery preserved.

## Planning your route

These ten sites span very different climates, languages and accessibility levels. Group them by region, allow a full half-day for each underground descent, and be ready for cool temperatures (around 8–12°C in most galleries). Local guides are often retired miners — their commentary is the single best part of any visit.
