American mining built towns, moved mountains, and left behind some of the most
dramatic industrial heritage in the world. These ten mines are the ones worth
travelling for — most as visitor sites today.

## 1. Bingham Canyon, Utah

The largest human-made excavation on the planet, a copper open pit visible from
space, with a visitor overlook above the terraces.

## 2. Sixteen to One / Empire Mine, California

A preserved Gold Rush hard-rock mine in Grass Valley with miles of underground
workings and a state historic park above.

## 3. Homestake, South Dakota

Once the deepest mine in North America, now home to an underground physics
laboratory a mile down — mining turned science.

## 4. Quincy Mine, Michigan

A Keweenaw copper-country giant with a towering hoist house and a tram-and-tunnel
underground tour.

## 5. Sterling Hill, New Jersey

A zinc mine famous for fluorescent minerals — its underground rooms glow under
ultraviolet light.

## 6. Soudan Underground Mine, Minnesota

Minnesota's oldest and deepest iron mine, descended by cage half a mile to
preserved workings.

## 7. Argo Mine, Colorado

A Clear Creek gold mine and mill with a drainage-tunnel tour through the heart
of the Colorado mineral belt.

## 8. Old Hundred Gold Mine, Colorado

A high San Juan mountain mine entered by mine train through solid rock.

## 9. Berkeley Pit, Montana

A flooded copper pit and stark environmental landmark in Butte — mining
heritage as cautionary monument.

## 10. Kennecott, Alaska

A spectacular abandoned copper mill town in Wrangell–St. Elias, one of the best
preserved industrial ghost sites in the world.

## How American mine tourism works

Many former mines operate as state parks, national historic landmarks, or
private show mines with guided underground tours — often by tram, cage, or
on foot through original adits. Active mines like Bingham Canyon offer only
surface overlooks. Tours book out in summer; reserve ahead.

## Timing and conditions

Underground mines are a constant cool temperature year-round — bring a jacket
even in July. Mountain show mines (Colorado, Alaska) are seasonal, opening only
when roads clear. Allow buffer for remote access; Kennecott in particular is a
long, rough drive.

## Safety and respect

Stay with the guide, never enter abandoned workings independently (collapse and
bad-air hazards are lethal and real), wear the hard hat, and treat mineral
sites as non-collecting — taking specimens damages the resource and is often
illegal. These are working memorials to dangerous labour; treat them with
respect.
